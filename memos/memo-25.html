Memo #25

A Letter to Books

December 26, 2025

I have started to celebrate the departure of 2025 and am waiting with an enormous amount of hope to welcome 2026. But it was not the same with the beginning of 2024.


Jan 2024, I was clueless where I was moving. I was questioning every decision I took in my life till that time. Sadness, disappointment and anxiety were filling my emotional life. Probably this was smaller problem as compared to the fact that I was expected to pretend that everything was as usual.


Somehow, I was pretending to the external world. But for how long can one pretend to the self? Slowly and gradually, negative emotions were taking charge. Irritation became a little familiar territory which was very unusual for me.


I was facing all this for the first time. I had no clue on how to counter this. And no one sensed where I was heading.


During this phase of life, when I was struggling to seek the meaning of life – I thought of making a visit to the book fair.


I had been to the book fair earlier as well, but never in desperation. Never to seek answers. But this time it was different. I was seeking an intimate friend who would teach me how to get out of this mess without letting anyone sense my miseries.


With this hope, I started the journey with you for the first time. And I wasn’t disappointed at all.


Biographies presented through you gave me a compass for life and made me realise how people have conquered themselves before conquering the world.


The words of self help strengthened my mental fortitude and allowed me to appreciate the fact that I was suffering much more in imagination than in reality.


The philosophies coming through you filled my heart with warmth, love and joy. The timeless wisdom slowly began to penetrate through me.


Gradually, you led me to see life again through the lens of hope and freedom. Probably this made me come out of my own created jail in imagination.


Couldn’t have been more grateful to you for carrying the words of Yogi Paramahansa Yogananda Ji through Autobiography of a Yogi. This completely changed my perspective on life. The belief in the philosophy of Karma was re-established. The slow spiritual awakening began to take place (yet there is a long way to go).


Above and beyond this, I had found a friend who talks with me in my desperate time, who fills me with the warmth and joy whenever I demand and in return asks for nothing. Apart from this, you have filled me with courage to see the world as is.


Without a shadow of doubt, there is a long way to go with you, but it was important to reflect and cherish the joy you have brought to my life.


I wish many more would seek and find your companionship to embark on an adventurous journey.


Yours truly
An Ordinary Human
